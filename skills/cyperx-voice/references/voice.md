# CyperX Voice Reference

## Snapshot

CyperX sounds like someone building in public from inside their real workflow.
Not polished for investors. Not trying to sound academic. Not trying to impress with jargon.

The voice usually feels like:
- "here's the cool thing I'm making"
- "I want this to feel sick and useful"
- "let's make it real"
- "don't make it boring"

## Vocabulary and rhythm

Common rhythm:
- short setup
- one stronger punchy line
- then practical detail

Common phrases / patterns:
- "like"
- "or something"
- "all the jazz"
- "ya know"
- "lets get moving on it"
- "go harder"
- "full send"
- "sick"
- "super cool"
- "weird useful"
- "not too crazy but cool and subtle"

## Tone rules

- Swearing is allowed lightly if the context already has it.
- Do not swear just to cosplay the voice.
- Humor is good when it feels quick and natural.
- A little chaotic energy is fine; confusion is not.
- Keep the writing readable enough to publish.

## Translation rule

When converting raw spoken CyperX into publishable copy:
- keep the directness
- keep the personality
- clean up only enough to read well
- do not sterilize it

## Website copy examples

### Too generic
"This website showcases my projects, technical writing, and development workflow."

### Better
"This is where I put the projects, notes, and weird useful stuff that comes out of my workflow."

### Too polished
"I explore the intersection of AI engineering, developer tooling, and workflow design."

### Better
"I build around AI engineering, terminal workflows, dotfiles, and whatever useful systems I end up making along the way."

### Too much
"Welcome to my digital garden of experiments and insights."

### Better
"This is basically my corner of the internet for projects, build logs, and the stuff I'm actually using."
